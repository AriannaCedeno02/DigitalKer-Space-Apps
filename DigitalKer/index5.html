Hubble Space Telescope

1Historical review

The telescope, named in honor of the American astronomer Edwin Hubble, was placed in orbit on April 24, 1990.

2The Hubble

Hubble, which has an orbital period between 96 and 97 minutes and is located at an altitude of 593 km above sea level, has taken some of the most iconic and awe-inspiring space images, such as the "Pillars of Creation" . Hubble's successor will be the James Webb Space Telescope, scheduled to launch in 2020.
