The Apollo missions and the arrival to the Moon

1Apolo 11

The Soviet Union again overtook the United States when Yuri Gagarin converted on April 12, 1961 in the first human being to travel to outer space, a month before the American Alan Shepard repeated the feat.

2Historic moment

That same year President John F. Kennedy delivered his famous speech: "I believe that this nation must commit to achieving, before the end of the decade, the goal of putting a man on the Moon and returning him safely to Earth. "

3the beginning

The Apollo program began two years later, but its beginnings were marked by tragedy. Apollo 1 never came to fruition. A fire in the module command during tests on the launch pad killed the three crew members, the astronauts, Vigil Grisson, Edward White and Roger Chaffee, on January 27, 1967.
