International Space Station

1Space station

The ISS is a joint project between the United States, Russia, Japan, the European Space Agency and the Canadian Space Agency. The first part of the station was launched in 1998, and the last module was placed in 2011.

2Astronauts

More than 200 astronauts from 18 countries have visited the station, which is divided into two sections, the Russian Orbital Segment and the American Orbital Segment. Both sections have financing until 2024.

3Did you know...?

The station is 109 meters long and 88 meters wide. It orbits the Earth every 91 minutes at an altitude of 400 km. This means that in 24 hours ISS astronauts see 16 sunrises and 16 sunsets..
